Cocktails

Each cocktail on this list has been made 20+ times. Enjoy!

  • Betelgeuse

    • Mezcal - Honey Simple Syrup - Beet Juice - Green Chartreuse - Lime Juice

    • 2oz mezcal (gold mezcal > silver)
    • 0.5oz honey simple syrup (1:1)
    • 0.75oz beet juice (fresh beets juiced from a juicer)
    • 0.25oz green chartreuse
    • 0.5oz freshly squeezed lime juice

    - Combine all ingredients into a shaker and shake with ice

    - Strain into a coupe glass

  • Tom Collins

    • Gin - Simple Syrup - Lemon Juice - Soda Water

    • 2oz gin (generally beefeater)
    • 0.75oz simple syrup (2:1 -- 2 parts sugar to 1 part water)
    • 1oz freshly squeezed lemon juice
    • 3oz soda water

    - Combine gin, simple syrup, and lemon juice into a shaker and shake with ice

    - Add soda water into the shaker and stir a few times

    - Strain into a glass filled with ice

    - (I stir the soda water after shaking because whenever I have added the soda water and shook the shaker, it causes a lot of pressure to build up and causes a mess)

  • Moscow Mule

    • Vodka - Ginger Simple Syrup - Lime Juice - Soda Water - Crushed Ice

    • 2oz vodka (generally titos)
    • 1.25oz ginger simple syrup (see Ginger Simple Syrup below for recipe)
    • 0.375oz freshly squeezed lime juice
    • 4oz soda water
    • ~1cup crushed ice

    - Crush ice and place into moscow mule mug or similar vessel

    - Combine remaining ingredients into vessel and stir vigorously

    - (most people would like 0.5oz lime but I don't like much lime)

  • Ginger Simple Syrup (not a cocktail)

    • Ginger - Turbinado Sugar - Water

    • 1lb fresh ginger
    • 2cups turbinado sugar
    • 2.5cups water

    - Peel 1lb of fresh ginger

    - Take 1/4 of the peeled ginger and grate it

    - Take remaining 3/4 peeled ginger and cut into 1/4 inch knobs

    - Place all peeled ginger, 1.5 cups of water, and 2 cups of turbinado sugar into a saucepan

    - Take a measurement (the best you can) of the liquid level (use back of spoon and mark level). This will be your final liquid level after simmering

    - Add remaining 1 cup of water to the saucepan

    - Set burner to medium heat and stir to combine so the sugar doesn't burn

    - Once the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is simmering, set to low heat and simmer until the liquid level you previously measured is reached (about 30 minutes)

    - Cut off the heat and let steep for an additional 30 minutes to 1 hour

    - Strain liquid through a fine sieve into clean glass jar

    - Should last about 3 weeks

  • Dirty Gin Martini

    • Gin - Olive Juice - Dry Vermouth

    • 2.25oz gin (tanqueray or hendricks)
    • 0.5oz olive juice (traditional olives with red pimento peppers in them, garlic mezzetta, or castlevetranos juice)
    • splash of dry vermouth (enough to wash glass)

    - Fill drinking vessel with ice and water and let rest to chill glass

    - Combine gin and olive juice into shaker with ice

    - Stir ingredients in shaker vigorously until very cold

    - Drain drinking vessel of ice water

    - Add splash of dry vermouth to vessel to lightly rinse the bottom half of the vessel

    - Pour dry vermouth out

    - Strain gin and olive juice mixture into vessel

    - Add two olives (my favorites are castlevetranos)
